Ashes to Ash, Holi's Sacred Dance in Varanasi

2025-03-11T06:12:00+00:00

Aghoris and Naga Sadhus celebrated Masane Ki Holi in Varanasi, marking the start of grand Holi festivities in the city.
The celebrations at Harishchandra Ghat saw the Sadhus and Aghoris smearing themselves with pyre ashes, symbolizing the cycle of life and death. Devotees engaged in meditation and danced to traditional drums as flames from cremation pyres flickered in the background.
A procession led by Baba Masan Nath, featuring drummers, artists, and hundreds of devotees, culminated at Harishchandra Ghat. Rituals were performed, prayers offered, and lamps lit to honor the spirits and seek divine blessings.
The event included a unique Holika Dahan site designed like a cremation ground in Pandeypur, depicting the essence of 'Masane Ki Holi'. Powerful chants and traditional Banarasi Nagaras created a surreal atmosphere during the celebrations.
Tourists and devotees from across India and beyond witnessed this unique Holi celebration that honored both life and death in the heart of Kashi.
