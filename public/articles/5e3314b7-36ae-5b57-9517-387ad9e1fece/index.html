Varanasi's Soul: Art Between Sleep and Awakening

2025-03-11T12:07:00+00:00

Artist Abhishek Pandey's solo exhibition, "Tandra," explores the impact of development on Varanasi's cultural landscape. Pandey, who grew up in Varanasi, critiques the modernization of the city, citing the widening of narrow lanes leading to the Markandika ghat and the modernization of the Dashashwamedh Ghat square. "Tandra," meaning the time between sleep and awakening, showcases a terracotta sculpture with drawings, symbolizing the transformation of Varanasi through construction.
The exhibition, curated by Sheena Maria Piedade, is divided into three sections representing the past, present, and future. Pandey, now living in Pune, reflects on technology's intrusion into the realm of invisible energies, particularly referencing Varanasi's traditions surrounding death and cremation.
